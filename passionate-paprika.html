🍳 01 — THE BEGINNING
Chapter One

Lockdown gave me a kitchen
and too much time. I made the most of it.

It started the way most good things do — out of necessity and a little boredom. During COVID lockdown in 2020, I found myself in the kitchen every single day, cooking experimental dishes, baking pastries, and trying things I'd always wanted to make but never had the time for.

I started posting it — not with a plan, just with genuine excitement. The response from the people around me was immediate and warm. They weren't just watching. They wanted to taste it.

"They weren't just watching.
They wanted to taste it."

🛍️ 02 — FROM BLOG TO BUSINESS
Chapter Two

When your followers become
your first customers.

Word spread through DMs and stories. People started placing orders for my pastries and baked goods. What began as a creative outlet became a real operation — I was handling content creation, order taking, baking, packaging, and delivery, all by myself.

No team. No agency. Just me, my kitchen, and an Instagram account.

✈️ 04 — A BIGGER DREAM
Chapter Four

Sometimes you have to close one chapter
to write a better one.

In 2022, I made the decision to move to London to pursue my Masters in Digital Marketing. Running a food business solo while navigating international student life on the other side of the world wasn't sustainable.

Passionate Paprika went quiet — not because the passion died, but because a bigger ambition called. Looking back, that decision gave me the marketing skills to do it properly the second time around.

"The passion never left.
The timing just wasn't right."
